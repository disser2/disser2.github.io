Galaxy Conquest

Expansion in a differentially rotating galaxy — how fast, and at which jump range?

Model

Each timestep your empire spends a fixed fleet capacity C on hyperspace jumps. Drives are rated for a fixed jump range D — every jump costs D, regardless of the actual hop. So you get K = ⌊C / D⌋ jumps per step, always to the nearest reachable unconquered systems. Short range: many jumps, but the frontier can stall until galactic rotation and orbital eccentricity shear new systems into reach. Long range: targets everywhere, but few jumps per step. Note: pure rotation preserves radii, so radial gaps only close through eccentric orbits.
